What is the Property Tax Digital Toolkit?
This toolkit is a one-stop-shop for all your needs for reforming property taxes in your State/ULB. It provides reform recommendations based on best-practices implemented nationally and internationally and provides a step-wise implementation guide that you can easily follow to take immediate action. Reform recommendations in each stage of the lifecycle of property tax have been framed as responses to issues and implementation challenges, sharply addressing WHAT NEEDS to BE DONE and HOW CAN IT BE DONE.
Current Practices

Enumeration means counting of properties for the purpose of taxation. In practical terms, this entails the creation and maintenance of a property register. Ideally, the process of enumeration should ensure completeness and accuracy of records.
It is important to have a complete record of properties in a jurisdiction to have an improved process of tax collection.

The process of enumeration has the following aspects:
1) Creation of property tax register

The ULB can create a physical record or a digital record of properties in their jurisdiction once counting is done.

2) Maintenance of property tax register

Property register can be updated by repeating a physical survey and/or it can be integrated with database of other departments in the municipal corporation.

Current Practices

Valuation refers to the methodology used for assigning values to all properties for the purpose of taxation. There are three methods for valuation of properties for the purpose of property tax which are currently used by States in India, i.e. the annual rental value method (ARV), unit area value method (UAV) and the capital value method (CV). These methodologies vary across States and cities, both in legislation and in practice.

Annual Rental Value (ARV) is the most widely used valuation method in Indian States.

The common aspect between ARV and UAV is that in most States that use these methodologies, the values are prescribed in an ad-hoc manner without clear linkage to underlying factors. The CV system, which is followed in a few States, directly links property tax to the prevailing guidance value as published by the Stamp Duties and Registration Department. However, the property value is generally depressed by virtue of several multiplicative factors that make the system non-buoyant. Moreover, property tax valuations are not revised regularly in tandem with increase in guidance values i.e. outdated guidance values may be used for property taxation. To overcome these challenges, all cities should adopt the CV system with minimum multiplicative factors and a provision for periodic increase linked to increase in guidance value.

Assessment of properties refers to the exercise of assessing the value of a particular property within the parameters defined by ULB or State for the purpose of taxation. The rules and formulae for assigning value to all properties within the city are defined by valuation. Assessment is the application of those rules and formulas to individual properties. Current Practices

Collection efficiency of property tax, which is an important own revenue indicator and is calculated as the ratio of tax collected to demand raised, is dependent on both completeness of billing and administrative efficiencies in the collection process. While many ULBs do not follow this, it is a good practice to ensure that all properties that have been assessed are billed, tax is collected in a timely manner, and periodic payment reminders are issued especially using electronic modes such as SMS and/or emails. The practice of maintaining manual records, which is the case for several ULBs in India, is also a key impediment to efficient billing & collection as it is vulnerable to errors. Significant staff vacancies in revenue departments also adversely impact collection efficiencies. Although some cities have done well to create online billing and collection systems through services of external vendors, they face difficulties in managing the system due to inadequate capacity building measures being undertaken for revenue officials. Online/digital payment of property tax is another good practice whose adoption has been relatively slow and varies across cities. Current Practices

Property Tax Management Information System (MIS) exists in all ULBs that have an online property register integrated with payment channels. However, the MIS systems have generally not been integrated with decision-making to improve coverage and collections. There are very few examples of States in which MIS systems are used by different levels of Municipal and State authorities for decision-making as well as monitoring and managing performance. Property tax collection and pendency (defaulters) data is also not available in public domain in majority of the ULBs.
